The circulatory system is one of the most vital systems in the human body. It consists of the heart, arteries, veins, and capillaries. Its primary role is to circulate blood and lymph throughout the body, providing essential substances to cells and removing waste.

The heart functions as the pump that maintains the flow of blood. Arteries carry oxygen-rich blood from the heart to the body, while veins return oxygen-poor blood back to the heart. Capillaries are small vessels where gas exchange occurs.
