Our Nanorobot Solution

If a nanorobot is created that can be injected into blood vessels, it will detect blood clots and release anticoagulant medication in a controlled manner, thus reducing side effects.

The nanorobot will be injected into a blood vessel and will follow the route until, with chemical sensors, it detects the substances associated with blood clot formation. Then, using proximity sensors, it will calculate the exact distance to release the medication in a safe and effective way.

Once its task is completed, the nanorobot will travel through the lymphatic system toward the digestive system and finally be naturally excreted from the body, ensuring minimal long-term impact.

Design Features:

  • Miniaturized chemical and proximity sensors
  • Controlled release mechanism for medication
  • Biodegradable components for safe excretion
  • Navigation through bloodstream and lymphatic pathways
